The Mission

At The Omni Project, we’re helping build the next generation of scholarly communications. The academic publishing system is deeply broken — the dissemination of research has become tightly intertwined with the commercial interests of publishers and the growing importance of reputation in academia.

We want to help break this industry deadlock, by disentangling research output from profit and prestige. The best way to achieve this is by giving academics the tools to collectively build an alternative model from the ground-up, which can allow a fundamentally better and more sustainable system to emerge.

Our vision for The Omni Project is to start bringing the world's academics closer together, leveraging the collective intelligence of academic communities, and scaling academic discussion to an unprecedented level. Afterall, this is what the Internet was originally intended for, and only then can academia re-align with its original purpose — to share new knowledge with the world.

Omni Platform

We're not creating another repository, portal, journal or publisher. We're helping build a community that cares about good quality research, where academics and scholars can contribute to anything and get rewarded for it. Being a good researcher on the platform means sharing, editing, reviewing and discussing research with others, and as long as you're making quality and consistant contributions, you're recognised as a good academic.

Omni is an open-source, distributed web application that enables scholars to write, share, discuss and review research papers with academic communities, without any intermediaries.

This essentially describes a "scholarly commons" where research is made as open and participatory as possible. Omni is a place for academics to freely share their research, and open it up to feedback and improvement from the community, regardless of what stage it's at. Every user is acknowledged for the contributions they make, and every piece of information shared is given a unique ID. This has the potential to drastically improve the flow of information, organisation of knowledge and evolution of ideas.

Omni is also pioneering new incentive and reputation systems for academics and scholars. By rewarding those for contributing value to the research process (rather than focusing solely on research outcomes) we can promote more valid, scientific and organic knowledge creation. Also, the technology that Omni is built on can allow the community to experiment with different currency models, in order to arrive at the most desirable, sustainable solution that works for everyone.

At a glance, Omni offers the following capabilities:

Write articles using an online editor, or upload your own files
Share articles with communities, get feedback and start discussions
Discover the best, most recent and controversial papers in your field
Review papers and help the community decide what valid research looks like
Manage your reputation, which reflects the quality and consistancy of your contributions
Powerful Features
Contextual information

Create your own reading experience by customising the left and right sidebars next to a paper. Choose whether to show the contents, comments, figures, appendices or references, or hide both sidebards for a clean experience. And each item in a contextual sidebar appears directly next to the part of the paper it's related to.

Manage Dynamic Papers

No paper is ever complete or final. Authors can write their papers within Omni, receive feedback from the community and easily make changes. Every edit is documented, archived and made transparent. Invite co-authors to collaborate, and even choose to keep your work offline until its ready to share.

Get feedback from day one

All papers on Omni are open to feedback from anyone in the academic community. Rather than writing a full peer-review, users can create discussion threads at various points in the paper. Readers can upvote/downvote comments, and also suggest edits or flag issues to the author (and if approved, are credited as contributors to the paper).

Engage with communities

Communities can form around any field or topic in academia, where research papers are assessed and reviewed according to their own measures of quality, validity and significance. Academics can follow the most recent, popular and controversial papers in a field, and discuss them with other like-minded people.

Manage your reputation

Omni uses a vast range of factors to determine your reputation: including the quality and consistancy of your articles, comments, edits and reviews. Your reputation can't grow without bound either; instead it reflects your overall ability to perform what the community considers important for an academic.

Who We're Helping

What types of academics and research papers are best suited for Omni? Who would benefit from using this platform? We're looking for people who are:

Frustrated with the frequently long and inefficient process to publication, and would prefer make their work as accessible as possible
Holding work which is unlikely to be published (or was rejected), such as replication studies, null results, and papers which aren't "flashy" or significant enough
Undertaking research in emerging or interdisciplinary fields, with a journal that isn't entirely relevant or doesn't have a broader enough audience
Interested in discussing research with peers and other academics in the community, outside of universities and conferences
Looking for an easier, faster way to review papers, rather than writing out lengthy responses in traditional peer-review
Involved with open-access journals (such as editors), and are looking to further the reach and impact of its published articles
Why Holochain?

We wanted Omni to be governed entirely its users, meaning no single entity can have control over the platform (including us). The Omni application will be hosted on a fully distributed, peer-to-peer protocol called Holochain. It's a truly decentralised, democratic and secure Internet, with the capacity for large-scale social coordination. Holochain is a new protocol for distributing web applications, as well as a crypto-accounting system for deep forms of wealth or value (such as Omni's system for tracking contributions). With Holochain, the academic community can become a fully autonomous organisation, without the need for a middleman or top-down incentive structure.

This technology is enabling us to build Omni on a system of value creation, compensation, transparency, accountability and community engagement (principles that academia *should* be built on). In summary, Holochain can enable the following:

Tracking and acknowledging all actions made by users
Protecting authorship and intellectual property rights
Rewarding anybody for creating or contributing value
Incentivising more productive and collaborative behaviour
Own your work

Holochain ensures that all users and data on Omni have digital signatures, meaning true authorship can always be verified. Authors are also protected under a Creative Commons license, and no longer have to relinquish their ownership rights to publishers.

Open source

We're building Omni as an open source application. We're inviting the community to help contribute to its development on Github, and any proposed changes to the application will be resolved democratically and by consensus.

Free to use, forever

We strongly believe in making knowledge and research as accessible as possible. Therefore, Omni will be completely free, but we can't work on this full time unless we have enough funding support.
